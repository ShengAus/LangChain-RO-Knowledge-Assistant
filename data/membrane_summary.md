# Membrane Performance Summary

Permeate flux is typically expressed in liters per square meter per hour, often written as LMH. It provides a normalized way to compare production rate across systems with different membrane areas.

Flux depends on transmembrane driving force, temperature, membrane condition, and fouling state. A high calculated flux is not automatically desirable if it exceeds the practical operating range for the selected element or feed water.

When reviewing flux, engineers often pair it with recovery and differential pressure trends to judge whether a system is being pushed too hard.
