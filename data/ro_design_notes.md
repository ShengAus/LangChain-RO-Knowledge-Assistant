# RO Design Notes

For a lightweight design review, start by checking feed flow, target permeate production, and expected recovery. These three values frame the mass balance and help identify whether the requested performance is realistic.

Recovery is commonly used as a quick indicator of how much feed becomes product water. A higher recovery target should always be considered together with fouling potential, scaling potential, and concentrate-side hydraulic limits.

Flux should be reviewed with membrane area and expected fouling rate in mind. Operating at moderate flux is often preferred for stable long-term performance, especially when feed quality varies.
