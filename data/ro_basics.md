# RO Basics

Reverse osmosis uses pressure to drive water through a semi-permeable membrane while rejecting most dissolved salts.

Key operating variables include feed pressure, feed water quality, temperature, recovery, and permeate flux. These variables interact, so a practical operating window is needed rather than a single target number.

Higher recovery can improve water efficiency, but it also increases concentration on the reject side and can raise scaling risk. Practical recovery is therefore limited by feed chemistry, pretreatment quality, and membrane operating limits.
